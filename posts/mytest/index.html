Mytest - oo

2021-06-06 764 words 4 minutes
Contents

Mods

  • In school writing essays is almost exclusive related to/and about English literature. Topics like: symbolism in Dickens, rather than the role of color in fashion.
  • This was due, according to Graham, to how the writing and the literature were introduced in high school (of course he talks about American educational system).
  • Regardless of its origin, the output of the unification of literature and composition is asking students to imitate what English professors have published in journals years before.

No defense

  • Despite school essays, the real essays do not need to take a position and defend it (to my knowledge, in the Italian education you do not have to defend your position, usually you are asked to provide a kind of overview).
  • This feature originated from medieval universities, where scholars were advocates, train to take side of an argument.
  • The problem with this approach is that you cannot change question to seek the truth, since your goal is to win
  • In this framework, the topic is chosen in advanced (it is your thesis), the paragraphs have to support the initial idea and the conclusions are the first statement rephrased.
  • Good writing requires right answers rather than right arguing.
  • In his drafts P.G. wants to know:
    • the boring parts (that he will cut)
    • the unconvincing bits (that we’ll be further elaborated but not better argued)
  • For smart readers, convincing arguments and true arguments become identical.

Trying

  • Essay derives from the French essayer, to try (see [[Michel de Montaigne]]’s book of essais).
  • essai is then an attempt (In Italian, saggio in the meaning of test to something, it derived from exigere, to examine. To not be confused with saggio/wise).

An essay is something you write to try to figure something out.

  • Now you do not have a thesis, but a question!
  • Montaigne’s discovery: Expressing ideas help to form them. Most of P.G. ideas come while he is writing. ==I can related to this feeling. Probably in the act of writing you manage to focus on the task and are able to shape thoughts in a way that opens new possibilities. It happened to me while writing my PhD thesis: wonderful ideas on how to improve things or to check something else. As said in the [[Kurzgesagt]]’s video [[A tail end]], while doing things you navigate life without many questions, you try to float.==
  • In an essay you are writing for yourself, you are thinking out loud.
  • The presence of an audience forces you to think well (like cleaning the house if you have guests)

The river

  • An essay needs also answer. If the starting question leads to a dead end, you do not publish the essay. Tell the reader something he did not already know.
  • The what is not important as long as it is interesting.
  • You are searching the truth not defending a position. Like a river, your path is longer than a straight line but it is the most economical route to the sea (in terms of potential(?) energy)
  • Algorithm of the river, for the writer: at each step flow down == flow interesting
    1. of all next places, choose the most interesting.
    2. from paragraph to paragraph, follow the ideas as they flow.
    3. if you hit a wall, backtrack and restart from an earlier point

  • An essay is not a reference work. Better to be interesting and unespected, than on a prescribed track.

Surprise

  • Essays should aim for maximum surprise.
  • Look for something you did not know, and that contradicts what you thought you know, i.e. surprises.
  • Finding such surprising is half of the work. You should find something you thought a lot and still surprises you.
  • Features:
    1. topic you thought a lot about
    2. ability to ferret out the inexpected

Observation

  • how do you learn how to ferret out the unexpected? Creating connections between different things. The more anomalies you have seen, better you are at discovering new ones. ==It makes me think about [[Mr. Rip]] live onf critical thinking, when he mentioned==

    ==Literacy of any sort gives you the power to recognize and unambigously label things that the illiterate can easily ignore as noise, fadd and bulshit.==

    ==from the [[Gervais Principle]]==

  • Habits to improve the odd of finding surprises:

    1. habit of asking questions, maily starting with why
    2. to discern among different why’s, prioritize what seems wrong
    3. among things that seems wrong, find the humorous ones, surprises makes us laugh.
    4. write down what surprises you

  • If you take yourself too seriously, you will avoid situations where your inexperience makes you look foolish. Doing so does not make you find surprises. this is a text lol
